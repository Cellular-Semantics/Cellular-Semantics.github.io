We gratefully acknowledge the funders who make our work possible.

## Chan Zuckerberg Initiative (CZI)

Supporting development and extension of the [Cell Ontology](/team/projects/cell-ontology/) and semantic tooling for single-cell data — including ontology coverage for Human Cell Atlas datasets, annotation of [CELLxGENE](https://cellxgene.cziscience.com/) content, our knowledge graph, and agentic ontology-editing and annotation workflows.

- **Current grant** (2026–2027) — Cell Ontology extension for published HCA atlases, agentic editing and annotation tools, and semantic support for CELLxGENE users.
- **CZI 2023 award** (recently completed) — added hundreds of new cell type terms, flagged Cell-Ontology-annotated content in CELLxGENE Census, released a Neo4j knowledge graph combining CL with CELLxGENE metadata, and delivered semantic query tooling.

## Allen Institute — Human and Mammalian Brain Atlas (HMBA)

Supporting ontology extensions for HMBA cell types and brain regions as part of the [BRAIN Initiative Cell Atlas Network (BICAN)](https://www.portal.brain-bican.org/) — including [Cell Ontology](/team/projects/cell-ontology/) terms for whole-mouse-brain and human/non-human-primate basal ganglia cell types, [brain anatomy ontologies](/team/projects/uberon/), and tools linking atlas annotations back to the Cell Ontology (2025–2026).

## Schmidt Foundation

Supporting our contributions to the [Cell Annotation Platform](https://celltype.info/) (CAP) — collaborative infrastructure for standardised cell type annotation, built around the [Cell Annotation Schema](/team/projects/standards-and-tools/).
