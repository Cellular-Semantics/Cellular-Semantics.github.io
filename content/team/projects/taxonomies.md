Working with the [BRAIN Initiative Cell Atlas Network (BICAN)](https://www.portal.brain-bican.org/), we build and maintain data-driven **cell-type taxonomies** of the mammalian brain. Each taxonomy is a structured, versioned classification of the cell types found in an atlas, maintained as a [Cell Annotation Schema](/team/projects/standards-and-tools/) taxonomy using the BICAN Taxonomy Development Tools. These taxonomies provide the bridge between raw single-cell data and the [Cell Ontology](/team/projects/cell-ontology/).

## Whole Mouse Brain taxonomy v1

A comprehensive cell-type taxonomy covering the entire mouse brain (CCN20230722), derived from the Allen Brain Cell Atlas and published in *Nature* (2024).

[GitHub](https://github.com/brain-bican/whole_mouse_brain_taxonomy)

## Whole Mouse Brain taxonomy v2 (consensus)

The second-generation **consensus** cell-type taxonomy of the whole mouse brain (CCN20251031), integrating data across sources into a single reconciled taxonomy.

[GitHub](https://github.com/brain-bican/whole_mouse_brain_consensus_taxonomy)

## Basal Ganglia — 3-species consensus

The HMBA cross-species **consensus** cell-type taxonomy of the primate basal ganglia (CS20250428), reconciling cell types across three species. It underpins the 2025 [cross-species consensus atlas of the primate basal ganglia](https://doi.org/10.64898/2025.12.15.694496).

[GitHub](https://github.com/brain-bican/basal_ganglia_consensus_taxonomy)

## Related anatomy ontologies

These taxonomies are accompanied by standard OBO ontology versions of the corresponding brain anatomies — see [Uberon](/team/projects/uberon/) for the Allen Brain Atlas ontologies (DHBA, HBA, MBA, HOMBA) and the [Basal Ganglia Ontology (BGO)](https://github.com/Cellular-Semantics/hmba_basal_ganglia_ontology).
